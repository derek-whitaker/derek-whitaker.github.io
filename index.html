What I do

Qualitative Research

As a formally trained anthropologist, I use qualitative methods to collect and analyze data that describes and explains the complexities that make up everyday life and the exceptional moments that periodically punctate it. I’m at ease with the following methods:

  • Conducting participant observation
  • Interviewing
  • Leading focus groups
  • Developing and administering questionnaires and surveys
  • Performing primary and secondary source research

Quantitative Research

As a quantitative researcher, I use mathematical models to analyze data, create stories and visualizations, and develop frameworks for proposing actionable recommendations based on my findings. I am comfortable employing a variety of Python libraries including:

  • Pandas
  • Matplotlib
  • Seaborn
  • Scikit-learn
  • TensorFlow
  • Keras

About me

Researcher based in the Washington D.C. area

I have a background in education and research. I hold a Bachelor of Arts in Anthropology from the University of Mary Washington (Fredericksburg, Virginia) and a Masters in Anthropology from the Université de Toulouse (Toulouse, France). I began coding as a hobby, but Python has since grown into a tool that I deploy in support of my social science research. In my free time, I continue deepening my understanding of Python, statistics, data analysis and visualization, and machine learning.

As a researcher, I have focused on the relationship between humans and their environments, with a particular interest in how space is created and managed to influence persons and their particular form of life. Much of my research has been centered on Western Europe and Latin America, where I have conducted ethnographic fieldwork. Regionally, I am also interested in francophone Africa, especially West Africa and the Maghreb. I have worked on and hope to continue exploring topics related to education, political and economic anthropology, interethnic relations, globalization, urban dynamics, technology, and environmental anthropology.
