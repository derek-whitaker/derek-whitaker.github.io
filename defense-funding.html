Streamlining Defense Funding Requests

Transformed a failing email-based funding system into a unified digital platform now used by 20+ Department of Defense agencies.

The Challenge

A Department of Defense agency responsible for distributing critical financial resources to support national security and foreign policy initiatives was drowning in operational chaos. Their entire funding request process—from submission to approval—ran through email.

The result? Anxiety, frustration, duplicated efforts, frequent data loss, and zero accountability. Poor coordination rippled across the DoD. As one stakeholder put it: "The center of the funnel is starting to break."

The Solution

Working with finance and policy personnel from 14 DoD agencies, our team used Agile and Human-Centered Design practices to investigate coordination issues, validate user needs, and rapidly iterate toward a solution.

Through research and stakeholder collaboration, we developed a comprehensive workflow model to centralize and manage the entire request lifecycle—from creation to approval. This model became the foundation for our digital tool.

We delivered a customized platform that enables users to:

  • Create and submit funding requests with standardized templates
  • Coordinate and route requests through proper channels
  • Review, adjudicate, and track request status in real-time
  • Search historical requests and maintain centralized data
  • Configure custom workflows for different request types

The tool brought structure, transparency, and accountability to a process that had operated in the dark for years.

The Impact

Within one year of launch, the platform was adopted by more than 20 DoD agencies—marking the first successful digitalization of a process at this scale for the client.

The new system now:

  • Improves request quality through standardization
  • Centralizes data and eliminates information silos
  • Enhances accessibility across the DoD community
  • Increases accountability and adherence to established protocols
  • Reduces coordination time and eliminates duplicate efforts

What started as a breaking system became a model for how critical government processes can be modernized through thoughtful design and user-centered practices.
